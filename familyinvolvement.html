Volunteer Hours

The Enrollment and Tuition Agreement at Berkwood Hedge includes a commitment from each family to contribute 40 hours of work to the school per year.  Single parents/guardians are asked to contribute 20 hours per year. This commitment not only helps keep tuition more affordable but also involves parents/guardians in the life of the school as volunteers in the classroom, on field trips, on the grounds, in the office, at events, and with fundraising projects.  Ten of the 40 hours (or five of the 20 hours), must be devoted to school-wide development activities.

Formerly, we called these "Fundraising Hours" but have renamed them "Development Hours" to encompass more than fundraising activities, including activities related to alumni, friends and family, and community relations.

Ways to Earn Development Hours

Committee Participation

Development Committee membership.  Spring Celebration Committee membership.

EVENTS

Fall Telling Tales Storytelling Festival: mailings, errands, publicity, set-up the day before the event, working the day of the event - any jobs associated with the festival including hours on the pre-Festival clean-up on the Saturday before the event.

Spring Celebration & Auction: donation solicitation, mailings, errands, picking up donations, participation on sub-committees (decorations, food, etc.), database entry, working during the event, writing thank you notes, invitation design, etc.

Grandparents/Grandfriends events and projects: mailings, errands, working during the event

PROJECTS

  1. Annual Fund Drive:  mailings
  2. Newsletter:  photography, writing
  3. Grant Writing:  writing and submitting
  4. Capital Campaign:  design, mailings, errands, event help
  5. Alumni Outreach:  design, mailings, errands, event help
  6. Development Jobs:  graphic design, mailings, copying, photography/video
eScrip

Participation in the eScrip program: You earn one Development hour for each person you sign-up (yourself included).  Example: One hour earned to sign myself up (all of my credit cards – not one hour per card) and one hour earned when I sign my mother up (all her credit cards – not one hour per card).

The remaining hours may be spent in any number of activities.  Contact your child(ren)'s classroom teacher(s), your Class Parents, or the office for work commitment ideas.  There are several school committees (see pages 29-31) where participation counts for work commitment hours.  We encourage you to earn some of your hours (maximum of three) by attending our parent education events.

Tracking Work Hours

Each family is responsible for listing completed work hours on a form posted on a clipboard in their child's classroom. If you have more than one child at Berkwood Hedge, please note the hours on the form in your younger child's classroom only. Remember to post your hours as you complete them.

Parents/guardians chaperoning overnight field trips can record 6 hours per day.

Classroom Volunteer Opportunities

Parent/guardian participation varies from class to class. Teachers will discuss volunteer opportunities at the Fall Curriculum Nights.

Fees in Lieu of Hours

At the end of the school year, unfinished work commitment hours will be billed at an hourly rate of $20.00.
